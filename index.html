Arequipa, Peru

Arequipa is a wonderful city surrounded by volcanoes and has incredible cuisine! Arequipa, known as the "White City," is the second most populous and important city in Peru. Located in the south of the country, its historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage Site thanks to its impressive colonial architecture built from sillar (white volcanic stone) and its traditional picanterías (traditional restaurants). Arequipa's cuisine has become famous for being one of the most varied and flavorful in Peru. In fact, most restaurants and traditional eateries in the region prepare a special dish for each day of the week: chaque on Monday, chairo on Tuesday, chochoca on Wednesday, chupe colorado or chuño on Thursday, chupe de viernes on Friday, puchero or timpusca on Saturday, and adobo arequipeño on Sunday. It also features typical dishes such as chupe de camarones (shrimp chowder), ocopa arequipeña (a potato and pepper sauce), solterito (a salad), rocoto relleno (stuffed rocoto peppers), picante de camarones (spicy shrimp stew), and desserts like queso helado (a type of ice cream).
